Cardiac Conditions

Understanding different heart conditions is the first step toward prevention and treatment. Here are the most common cardiovascular diseases.

Most Common

Coronary Artery Disease

Plaque buildup in coronary arteries reduces blood flow to the heart, potentially leading to angina or heart attack.

Learn More

Heart Failure

The heart can't pump enough blood to meet the body's needs, causing fatigue, shortness of breath, and swelling.

Learn More

Arrhythmia

Abnormal heart rhythms that can be too fast, slow, or irregular, potentially leading to serious complications.

Learn More

Warning Signs

Recognizing these symptoms early can save lives. Don't ignore these potential signs of heart trouble.

Chest Discomfort

Pressure, tightness, or pain in the chest that may come and go (angina) or be constant (heart attack).

Read More

Breathing Difficulties

Shortness of breath during routine activities or while at rest may indicate heart problems.

Read More

Unexplained Fatigue

Extreme tiredness that isn't relieved by rest can be a sign of heart disease, especially in women.

Read More

Cardiac Emergency?

If you or someone else experiences these symptoms for more than 5 minutes:

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness
CALL 911

Immediate treatment can save lives - don't delay!

Prevention Strategies

Up to 80% of premature heart disease can be prevented through lifestyle changes. Here's how to protect your heart.

Heart-Healthy Nutrition

Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins and healthy fats. Limit sodium, added sugars and processed foods.

View Meal Plans

Physical Activity

150 minutes weekly of moderate exercise strengthens your heart, improves circulation and helps maintain healthy weight.

Exercise Guides

Stress Reduction

Chronic stress contributes to heart disease. Practice mindfulness, meditation, yoga or other relaxation techniques.

Stress Relief Tips

Treatment Options

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about heart health and cardiovascular conditions.

Early warning signs can include:

  • Chest discomfort (pressure, tightness, pain)
  • Shortness of breath during routine activities
  • Palpitations or irregular heartbeat
  • Unexplained fatigue or weakness
  • Swelling in feet, ankles or legs
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness

Many people experience no symptoms until a serious event occurs, which is why regular check-ups are important.

Recommendations vary by age and risk factors:

  • Age 20-39: Every 4-6 years if low risk
  • Age 40-54: Every 2-4 years
  • Age 55+: Every 1-2 years
  • High risk individuals: Annually or as recommended by your doctor

Risk factors include high blood pressure, diabetes, smoking, obesity, family history of heart disease, and sedentary lifestyle.

While existing damage to the heart muscle cannot be completely reversed, the progression of heart disease can often be slowed, stopped, or even partially reversed through:

  • Comprehensive lifestyle changes (diet, exercise, stress management)
  • Medications to control risk factors
  • Cardiac rehabilitation programs
  • In some cases, surgical interventions

Studies show that aggressive lifestyle modifications can reduce plaque buildup in some patients.

Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction):

  • Occurs when blood flow to part of the heart is blocked
  • The heart usually continues to beat
  • Symptoms include chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea
  • Medical emergency requiring immediate treatment

Cardiac Arrest:

  • Occurs when the heart suddenly stops beating effectively
  • Patient loses consciousness and stops breathing
  • Requires immediate CPR and defibrillation
  • Can be caused by a heart attack but is a distinct event
