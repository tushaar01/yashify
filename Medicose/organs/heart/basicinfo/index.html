Heart Chambers

  • Right Atrium: Receives deoxygenated blood from body via superior/inferior vena cava
  • Right Ventricle: Pumps blood to lungs through pulmonary artery
  • Left Atrium: Receives oxygenated blood from lungs via pulmonary veins
  • Left Ventricle: Thickest chamber; pumps blood to entire body through aorta

Wall Thickness: LV (10-15mm) > RV (3-5mm) > Atria (2-3mm)

Heart Valves

Valve Location Function
Tricuspid Between RA and RV Prevents backflow during ventricular contraction
Pulmonary Between RV and pulmonary artery Prevents blood from flowing back into RV
Mitral (Bicuspid) Between LA and LV Allows one-way blood flow to LV
Aortic Between LV and aorta Prevents backflow into LV after contraction

Heart Wall Layers

  • Pericardium: Double-walled sac (fibrous + serous) that protects and anchors heart
  • Myocardium: Thick muscular middle layer (cardiac muscle tissue)
  • Endocardium: Smooth inner lining that reduces friction

Pulmonary Circulation

Pathway: Right Ventricle → Pulmonary Arteries → Lungs → Pulmonary Veins → Left Atrium

  • Only time arteries carry deoxygenated blood
  • Blood gets oxygenated in lung capillaries
  • CO2 is exchanged for O2 during this process

Systemic Circulation

Pathway: Left Ventricle → Aorta → Arteries → Capillaries → Veins → Vena Cava → Right Atrium

  • Delivers oxygen and nutrients to all body tissues
  • Removes CO2 and waste products
  • Longer pathway than pulmonary circulation

Coronary Circulation

Supplies blood to the heart muscle itself

  • Left Coronary Artery: Supplies LV, LA, and interventricular septum
  • Right Coronary Artery: Supplies RA, RV, and SA/AV nodes
  • Blockages cause myocardial infarction (heart attack)

Electrical Conduction System

  1. SA Node (Sinoatrial Node): Pacemaker (60-100 bpm)
  2. AV Node (Atrioventricular Node): Delays impulse (0.1s)
  3. Bundle of His: Conducts to ventricles
  4. Purkinje Fibers: Rapid ventricular contraction

ECG Waves: P wave (atrial depol), QRS complex (ventricular depol), T wave (ventricular repol)

Preventive Measures

  • Diet: Mediterranean diet, reduce salt/sugar
  • Exercise: 150 mins moderate exercise weekly
  • Avoid: Smoking, excessive alcohol
  • Monitor: BP (<120/80), Cholesterol (LDL <100), BMI (<25)

Common Heart Conditions

Disease Causes Symptoms
Coronary Artery Disease Plaque buildup in arteries Chest pain, shortness of breath
Arrhythmia Electrical system problems Irregular heartbeat, dizziness
Heart Failure Weakened heart muscle Fatigue, fluid retention

Heart Disease Statistics

  • Leading cause of death globally (17.9M deaths/year)
  • 1 in 3 adults has hypertension
  • 80% of premature heart disease is preventable
